TrueNetwork at a Glance

TrueNetwork is Canada’s first integrated platform mapping the country’s non-profit and charitable landscape. It connects non profit organizations, funders, provincial and local governments, policymakers, and researchers across shared missions: building a unified network of knowledge, collaboration, and impact.

Guided by Canada’s 2030 Agenda: Sustainable Development Goals (17 SDGs)

TrueNetwork aligns Canada’s non-profit sector with “Moving Forward Together: Canada’s 2030 Agenda National Strategy.” Built on Canada’s sustainable development framework, its goals, targets, and indicators, TrueNetwork uses cutting-edge data technology to reveal how collective community action contributes to both national and global objectives. The platform promotes transparency, efficiency, and collaboration across the sector, helping organizations measure, connect, and amplify their impact.

Explore. Analyze. Collaborate.
In its current release, TrueNetwork integrates community knowledge from over 137,000 Canadian charities, including nearly 85,000 officially registered as of October 2025, bringing together organizational profiles, reports, handbooks, and toolkits created by organizations nationwide.
Applying the Canadian SDG framework, TrueNetwork classifies resources and organizations under relevant goals and themes. Equipped with intelligent search and analytics tools, the platform enables you to instantly explore documents, organizations, funders, and collaborators, uncover collaboration clusters, and build on verified, reusable insights that strengthen collective problem-solving across Canada’s non-profit sector.

This is just the beginning: more features and deeper insights are on the way as TrueNetwork grows to represent the full spectrum of Canada’s non-profit ecosystem.

Why Sustainble Development Goals (SDGs)?

Beyond CRA, ICNPO and NAICS classification systems

Through our research, we reviewed the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) charity categories, the International Classification of Non-Profit Organizations (ICNPO) schema, and the North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) Canada. We found that these classification systems are primarily designed for economic or administrative purposes, rather than serving as goal-oriented frameworks aligned with a sustainable vision.

While TrueNetwork plans to integrate these coding systems alongside the SDG framework, we believe that non-profit organizations should also be classified in a goal-based, impact-driven way , within a framework that supports the quality of life and sustainability goals of Canadians.

Our review highlights gaps, especially for cross-cutting initiatives such as advocacy for sustainable transportation. These taxonomies remain valuable, but connecting Canada’s non-profit sector to national and global frameworks (SDGs) provides clearer alignment, comparability, and visibility of real-world impact.

Reflecting real-world diversity

Many non-profits are multi-activity organizations, and single primary or secondary codes (CRA/ICNPO/NAICS) often fail to capture that complexity. Because the SDG framework is goal-oriented with clear targets and indicators, organizations and their community-generated knowledge can be classified under multiple SDG goals that they advance within Canadian communities.

The Canadian SDG framework allows us to tag specific contributions across goals, capturing the full breadth of programs while linking them to Canada’s national strategies. This approach helps non-profits find and connect with peers who share similar focus areas, fostering stronger networks locally and across Canada.
